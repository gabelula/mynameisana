"To the District Attorneys, and officials within the Department of Justice and TriMet, involved with the case of Ana del Rocio,

The recent judgement in the case of Ana del Rocio is correct. A policy of sweeping fare enforcement violate the constitutional rights of transit riders. It is a waste of resources to pursue charges against transit riders for a failure to show proof of fare. Rather than pursue charges against Ana, we the undersigned believe this moment is an opportunity to respond with renewed focus on truly protecting and serving the public. Pursuit of this charge, and others like it, is a waste of public resources. The correct response to this judgement is to accept it. We encourage TriMet to invest in further fare relief, rather than the further militarization or enclosure of public transit infrastructure, or criminalization of transit riders. The DAs and DOJ should let this ruling stand."

Ana del Rocio

"Ana’s name is Ana, and her name is well known. She is a community leader and has enjoyed a successful political career. A name that only a handful of people know is Rosa Valderrama. For good reason: this name, assigned to Ana at birth, is a painful reminder of a dark time in Ana’s early life. Her legal name has not yet been changed to match the name she uses throughout her life: Ana del Rocío. But this is not a crime." “What’s Your Name?” - April 2018
